IRS PUBLICATION 17 - YOUR FEDERAL INCOME TAX (2024 TAX YEAR)
Excerpted key sections for reference

================================================================
STANDARD DEDUCTION AMOUNTS (2024)
================================================================
- Single or Married Filing Separately: $14,600
- Married Filing Jointly or Qualifying Surviving Spouse: $29,200
- Head of Household: $21,900

Additional Standard Deduction (age 65 or older / blind):
- Single: +$1,950
- Married Filing Jointly: +$1,550 per qualifying person

================================================================
TAX BRACKETS 2024 (Single Filers)
================================================================
10%  : $0 – $11,600
12%  : $11,601 – $47,150
22%  : $47,151 – $100,525
24%  : $100,526 – $191,950
32%  : $191,951 – $243,725
35%  : $243,726 – $609,350
37%  : Over $609,350

================================================================
TAX BRACKETS 2024 (Married Filing Jointly)
================================================================
10%  : $0 – $23,200
12%  : $23,201 – $94,300
22%  : $94,301 – $201,050
24%  : $201,051 – $383,900
32%  : $383,901 – $487,450
35%  : $487,451 – $731,200
37%  : Over $731,200

================================================================
RETIREMENT CONTRIBUTION LIMITS (2024)
================================================================
- 401(k) / 403(b) employee contribution limit: $23,000
- 401(k) catch-up contribution (age 50+): additional $7,500
- Traditional IRA contribution limit: $7,000
- IRA catch-up contribution (age 50+): additional $1,000
- IRA deductibility phases out for single filers with workplace plan: $77,000 – $87,000 MAGI
- IRA deductibility phases out for MFJ with workplace plan: $123,000 – $143,000 MAGI

================================================================
COMMON ABOVE-THE-LINE DEDUCTIONS (Form 1040 Schedule 1)
================================================================
- Student loan interest: up to $2,500 (phases out $80,000–$95,000 single / $165,000–$195,000 MFJ)
- Educator expenses: up to $300 ($600 MFJ both educators)
- Health Savings Account (HSA) contributions: $4,150 (self-only) / $8,300 (family coverage)
- Self-employed health insurance premiums: 100% deductible
- Self-employment tax deduction: 50% of SE tax paid

================================================================
ITEMIZED DEDUCTIONS (Schedule A)
================================================================
State and Local Taxes (SALT):
- Capped at $10,000 ($5,000 MFS) for combined state income/sales and property taxes

Mortgage Interest:
- Deductible on loans up to $750,000 (loans originated after Dec 15, 2017)
- Points paid on home purchase loan fully deductible in year paid

Charitable Contributions:
- Cash donations: up to 60% of AGI
- Property donations: up to 30% of AGI (FMV at time of donation)
- Must have written acknowledgment for any donation of $250 or more

Medical Expenses:
- Deductible to extent they exceed 7.5% of AGI
- Includes premiums for insurance not paid by employer, prescriptions, dental, vision

================================================================
CHILD AND DEPENDENT CARE CREDIT
================================================================
- Up to $3,000 qualifying expenses for 1 child; $6,000 for 2 or more
- Credit rate: 20%–35% depending on AGI
- AGI over $43,000 = 20% credit rate

================================================================
CHILD TAX CREDIT (2024)
================================================================
- $2,000 per qualifying child under age 17
- Refundable portion (Additional Child Tax Credit): up to $1,700
- Phase-out begins at $200,000 AGI (single) / $400,000 (MFJ)

================================================================
EARNED INCOME TAX CREDIT (EITC) 2024
================================================================
- 0 children: max credit $632, income limit ~$18,591 (single)
- 1 child: max credit $4,213, income limit ~$49,084 (single)
- 2 children: max credit $6,960, income limit ~$55,768 (single)
- 3+ children: max credit $7,830, income limit ~$59,899 (single)

================================================================
SELF-EMPLOYMENT TAX
================================================================
- SE tax rate: 15.3% on net SE income up to Social Security wage base ($168,600 for 2024)
- 2.9% Medicare tax on all net SE income above $168,600
- Additional 0.9% Medicare surtax on SE income above $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (MFJ)

================================================================
ESTIMATED TAX PAYMENTS
================================================================
- Required if expected tax liability exceeds $1,000 after withholding
- Due dates: April 15, June 17, September 16, January 15
- Safe harbor: pay 100% of prior year tax liability (110% if prior year AGI > $150,000)

================================================================
CAPITAL GAINS TAX RATES (2024)
================================================================
Long-term capital gains (assets held >1 year):
- 0%  : Taxable income up to $47,025 (single) / $94,050 (MFJ)
- 15% : Taxable income $47,026–$518,900 (single) / $94,051–$583,750 (MFJ)
- 20% : Taxable income above those thresholds

Short-term capital gains: taxed as ordinary income

Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT): 3.8% on lesser of net investment income or
excess of MAGI over $200,000 (single) / $250,000 (MFJ)

================================================================
PENALTIES AND COMPLIANCE
================================================================
- Failure to file penalty: 5% of unpaid tax per month, max 25%
- Failure to pay penalty: 0.5% of unpaid tax per month, max 25%
- Accuracy-related penalty: 20% of underpayment due to negligence or substantial understatement
- Substantial understatement: understating tax by more than 10% of correct tax or $5,000

================================================================
HOME OFFICE DEDUCTION (Self-Employed)
================================================================
- Simplified method: $5 per square foot, up to 300 sq ft (max $1,500)
- Regular method: actual expenses × (home office sq ft / total home sq ft)
- Space must be used regularly and exclusively for business

================================================================
VEHICLE DEDUCTION (Business Use)
================================================================
- Standard mileage rate 2024: 67 cents per mile for business
- Actual expense method: deduct percentage of actual costs equal to business use %
- Must maintain mileage log with date, destination, business purpose
